\begin{verse}[\versewidth]
\poemlines{5}
So good luck came, and on my roof did light,\footnotetext{$\xi$ `The comming of good luck', Rev Robert Herrick (1591 -- 1674). \cite{obev}.}\\*
Like noiseless snow, or as the dew of night:\\
Not all at once, but gently, as the trees\\*
Are by the sunbeams tickled by degrees.
\end{verse}
